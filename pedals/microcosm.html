Hologram Microcosm

Granular · Glitch · Looper — how it works

Granular

The Microcosm chops your incoming signal into tiny slices called grains and replays, layers, pitches and scatters them in time to build evolving ambient, glitchy and rhythmic textures. It bundles 11 effect styles across four families, a synth-style resonant filter, a built-in reverb (Space), a hold/sampler, and a 60-second looper — all syncable to tempo.

Grains

What granular actually means

Each dot is a grain — a tiny slice of your sound. Higher Activity spawns more, denser grains; they scatter in time and pitch to form a cloud.

How busy the engine is — from sparse, occasional grains to a thick, continuous swarm.

Tweaks grain length and how far they're flung in pitch/time — small & tight vs. long & smeared.

Engine

The effect families

The subdivision locks the grain/loop timing to a note value of the clock (Tap Tempo or MIDI), so the texture stays in time with the track.

Controls

Shaping the texture

Reflections

Feeds the processed sound back into itself so grains/loops regenerate and build. Higher = longer, denser, more self-sustaining clouds.

Space

A built-in reverb that smears everything into a wash. A little adds depth; a lot turns the output into pure ambient atmosphere.

Filter

A synth-style resonant low-pass for dramatic tone sculpting — sweep it for filter-swept, evolving movement across the texture.

Mix

Dry vs. wet balance. Blend in subtle texture under your playing, or go fully wet and let the Microcosm become the instrument.

Capture

Looper & Hold

60-second looper

Capture phrases with one tap, with infinite overdubbing, reverse playback, fade in/out and undo/erase. It can sit before the effects (process the loop) or after (loop the wet output) — or run independently.

Hold / sampler

Freezes the effect in its current state and sustains it indefinitely. Play melodies on top of the frozen drone, then release to let it go. Pair with Tap Tempo / MIDI sync and the 16 presets for live recall.
