Reverb

Space — the sound of a room around the signal

Space

Reverb is what you get when sound bounces off thousands of surfaces in a space and arrives at your ears as a dense, decaying cloud of reflections. Unlike a delay's distinct repeats, reverb is a smooth wash of tails that places your sound in a room — from a tight booth to a vast cathedral.

Concepts

The essentials

Size / Decay (RT60)

How long the tail takes to fade out — small rooms decay fast, halls and cathedrals ring for seconds. The single biggest "space" control.

Pre-delay

The gap before the reverb starts. A little pre-delay keeps the dry sound clear and upfront while the tail blooms behind it — great for vocals.

Damping

How quickly high frequencies fade in the tail. Soft, absorbent rooms (carpet, curtains) damp highs fast; hard tile/stone rooms stay bright.

Early reflections vs. tail

Early reflections (the first bounces) tell your ear the room's shape/size; the diffuse tail is the dense wash that follows. Together they define the space.

Types: room, hall, plate, spring

Plates are smooth & dense (classic on vocals/snares); springs are boingy & vintage (the Volante has one); rooms/halls model real acoustic spaces.

Mix & sends

Reverb is usually run on a send so several sources share one space. Keep it lower than you think — a little glues a mix; too much washes it out.
