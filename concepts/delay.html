All Concepts

Delay

Echoes — repeating the signal over time

Time

A delay records your signal and plays it back a moment later. Feed some of that repeat back into the input and it echoes again and again, each pass quieter than the last. Control the time between repeats, how many there are (feedback), and how much echo you hear (mix) and you have everything from slapback to ambient washes.

Concepts

The essentials

Delay Time

The gap between repeats. Sync it to tempo for musical echoes — short (~80ms) gives slapback, longer gives rhythmic trails.

Feedback

How much of the repeat is fed back in to echo again. More feedback = more repeats. Near 100% it self-oscillates into a runaway drone.

Mix / Wet-Dry

Balance of dry signal vs. echoes. Subtle blends add depth; heavy mixes push the echoes to the front for dub-style effects.

Digital vs. Analog vs. Tape

Digital repeats stay clean; analog (BBD) and tape repeats darken and degrade each pass — that warmth is why tape echoes like the Volante are loved.
